Drumming Page

A huge passion of mine since the very beginning has been music. I have been playing drums since I was 8 years old and am thankful to have had the opportunity to play with hundreds of different musicians across the southern New Hampshire area.

I have played in a number of original bands, such as the local-to-Lowell band Out Of Water, as well as a professional Led Zeppelin tribute band called Four Symbols. Apart from that, I play in the graduation band at Northern Essex Community College and was a member of my middle school's jazz band. I was also featured in a number of single-song performances for Mojo's Music in Salem, New Hampshire, and the Music Workshop (now closed), also in Salem.

Below are a few links to clips of me playing at different points in my life.

GitHub Project Page

I got into programming kind of late in my education. After getting my HiSET (GED) diploma, I initially opted to go into Information Technology as my major. I knew I liked computers, but the first person I spoke to at my school asked me, "Okay, you like computers. Well... do you like math?" Of course, I answered how any person who didn't finish high school would answer that question: "NO!" This person ruled out Computer Science for me and sent me on a path toward IT.

While on this IT path, I had to take a required programming class for Python. Right there, in what seemed on the surface to be a huge pain of a class, my love for programming was sparked, which led me to where I am today!

Below are some of the projects I am proud of. The primary languages I use are Python, Bash, and Arduino C as of now.

Projects

Welcome Terminal in Python This program was developed to greet me when I turn on my Linux machine with the weather of my area and a dad joke. It stores a list of greetings (good and bad) to ensure a unique greeting every day of the year! GitHub Link
Shortcut Manager in Java I made this project to help me save time with my schoolwork. At the time, I was taking a Data Structures class in Java and my first C++ course, so this tool could open a templated Word document or any of the IDEs I was using at the time. It also features a poorly constructed GUI in Java Swing, which shows why I need this class ASAP! GitHub Link
Star Blasters in Python This was the culmination of all my newly found passion for programming from that infamous "Programming for IT" class. I spent hours on this project before the class even started. I told myself I'd do everything I could to get a good grade for that class, and I knew the final was a "Show me what you know" style assignment. I was beyond proud of how it came out at the time, but I hope my current code shows just how far I've come since then. GitHub Link
